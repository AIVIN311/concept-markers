This domain serves as a reference point for examining scarcity in computational capacity.

Digital systems are often perceived as infinitely scalable. In practice, computation is constrained by energy, hardware, supply chains, and physical infrastructure.

As artificial intelligence, automation, and simulation expand, access to compute becomes a strategic resource.

Scarcity no longer centers solely on land, labor, or capital. It increasingly centers on processing power, data throughput, and energy-backed computation.

In such conditions, those who control computational capacity shape economic growth, technological progress, and governance capability.

This site does not argue that scarcity is artificial or temporary.

It marks a structural reality: computation is becoming a limited and contested resource at the core of future civilization.
