This domain serves as a reference point for examining how personhood may emerge across biological and synthetic systems.

Hybrid personhood does not assume a single substrate. It refers to conditions in which identity, agency, and legal recognition are distributed between human, machine, or institutional structures.

In such configurations, continuity and responsibility may no longer map cleanly onto one body or one system.

This site does not advocate a position.

It does not provide a framework, service, or policy proposal.

Its purpose is to mark a conceptual layer in which personhood becomes structurally hybrid rather than singular.

This page is intentionally minimal.

It exists to ensure the term Hybrid Personhood has a stable place to stand.
