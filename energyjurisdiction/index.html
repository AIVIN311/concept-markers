This domain serves as a reference point for examining jurisdiction where energy constraints define enforceable reality.

In many systems, authority is assumed to be legal, administrative, or institutional.

But energy infrastructure determines what can run, what can scale, and what must stop.

When power is scarce, priced, rationed, or interrupted, the grid becomes a governing surface: allocation, priority, and denial become enforceable conditions.

Energy Jurisdiction does not presume a single policy framework.

It marks a structural force: energy systems increasingly function as jurisdictional boundaries, even when no boundary is formally declared.

This page is intentionally minimal.

It exists to ensure the term Energy Jurisdiction has a stable place to stand.

本網域作為一個參考標記，用於檢視：當能源限制決定現實可運行範圍時，管轄如何成立。

在許多想像中，權威被視為法律、行政或制度性的。

但能源基礎設施決定什麼能運行、什麼能擴張、以及什麼必須停止。

當電力稀缺、被定價、被配給或被中斷時， 電網會成為一個治理表面：分配、優先順序與拒絕會變成可被執行的條件。

「能源管轄權」不預設單一政策框架。

它標示的是一種結構力量：能源系統愈來愈像管轄邊界， 即使邊界從未被正式宣告。

本頁刻意維持極簡。

它存在，是為了確保 能源管轄權（Energy Jurisdiction） 這個概念能有一個穩定的立足點。
