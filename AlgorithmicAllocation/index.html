This domain serves as a reference point for examining how resources are increasingly allocated by automated systems rather than negotiated by institutions or markets alone.

Algorithmic allocation does not operate through visible commands or public decisions.

It emerges through prioritization rules, optimization models, queue systems, eligibility criteria, and real-time access controls.

Energy, computing power, credit, bandwidth, housing, healthcare, and attention are progressively distributed by systems that rank, throttle, approve, or deny in real time.

In such environments, power is exercised not by owning resources directly, but by controlling the mechanisms that assign them.

This site does not advocate a position.

It does not provide a service, framework, or policy proposal.

Its purpose is to name a structural shift already operating across infrastructure, governance, and economic systems — often without explicit recognition.
