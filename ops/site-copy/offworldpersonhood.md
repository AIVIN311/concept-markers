## Body Copy

### EN

- This domain serves as a reference point for examining how personhood recognition changes when the subject is situated beyond Earth-centered jurisdiction.
- On Earth, personhood is typically stabilized through territorial law, civil registries, courts, and administrative routines that assume a planet-bound governance boundary.
- Offworld conditions disrupt these assumptions: distance, latency, infrastructure dependence, and fragmented authority make recognition harder to anchor to a single state or legal system.
- In such environments, when personhood recognition must move across terrestrial and non-territorial governance contexts, it requires jurisdictional interfaces and sovereignty anchors to remain coherent and enforceable.
- In such environments, personhood becomes less a default status and more a negotiated interface between identity, access, and operational control within constrained systems.
- The central question is not whether a being is “human” or “synthetic,” but which governance layer has the authority to recognize, deny, or suspend personhood claims in non-territorial space.
- When survival, mobility, and participation depend on habitats, stations, protocols, and platforms, recognition can be enforced through access control rather than through deliberation or due process.
- Offworld personhood therefore highlights a structural risk: the same systems that sustain life can also define who counts, who speaks, and who remains legible as a subject.
- This site does not propose legal regimes, space policy frameworks, or institutional designs for extraterrestrial citizenship.
- Its purpose is to name an emerging governance condition in which personhood recognition is shaped by orbital infrastructures, jurisdictional drift, and operational constraints. This page deliberately maintains minimalism so the term Offworld Personhood can retain a stable foothold as off-Earth governance systems continue to form.

### ZH

- 本網域作為一個參考標記，用於檢視：當主體處於地球中心法域之外時，人格承認如何改變。
- 在地球上，人格通常透過領土法律、戶籍登錄、法院與行政慣例被穩定下來，並預設治理邊界與法權範圍是「行星內」的。
- 然而，離地條件會打破這些預設：距離與延遲、對基礎設施的依賴、以及權威的碎片化，使承認難以被錨定在單一國家或法律系統上。
- 在這樣的環境裡，當人格承認需要跨越地表與非地表治理情境時，它需要管轄介面與主權錨點，才能保持一致並具有可執行性。
- 在這樣的環境裡，人格更像是一個被協商的介面：介於身份、存取權與運作控制之間，而不再是預設自動成立的地位。
- 核心問題不在於主體是「人類」或「合成」，而在於：在非領土空間中，哪一層治理結構有權承認、拒絕或暫停人格主張。
- 當生存、移動與參與依賴棲居地、太空站、協定與平台時，承認可能透過存取控制被執行，而非透過審議或正當程序被建立。
- 因此，離地人格凸顯一種結構性風險：維持生命的系統，同時也可能界定誰算數、誰能發聲、誰能持續被視為可辨識的主體。
- 本站不提出太空法制度、政策框架或「離地公民」的設計方案。
- 它的目的，是為一種正在形成的治理條件命名：人格承認將被軌道基礎設施、法域漂移與運作限制共同塑形。本頁刻意維持極簡，使 離地人格（Offworld Personhood） 這個詞在離地治理系統仍在生成時，仍能保有穩定立足點。
