This domain serves as a reference point for examining how reserve systems evolve when state-issued fiat currency no longer monopolizes trust.

Post-fiat reserve systems do not require a single replacement currency.

They form through hybrid anchors: commodities, strategic resources, sovereign instruments, digital assets, and settlement infrastructures that distribute backing across layers.

In such architectures, reserve status becomes modular, contested, and periodically reweighted under stress, liquidity shifts, and geopolitical constraint.

This site does not propose a new monetary order.

Its purpose is to mark the transition in which “what counts as reserves” becomes a design question rather than a settled assumption.

在這種架構中，儲備地位會變得模組化、可爭奪，並在壓力、流動性變化與地緣約束下被反覆調整權重。
