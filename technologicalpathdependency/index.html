This domain serves as a reference point for examining how early technological choices constrain or shape future possibilities.

Technological path dependency does not require optimal design. It emerges through adoption, standardization, infrastructure investment, and compatibility requirements that favor continuity over reconsideration.

As systems scale, reversal becomes increasingly costly, even when superior alternatives exist.

This site does not promote innovation strategies or intervention models.

Its purpose is to name a structural dynamic that operates across platforms, institutions, and societies — often perceived as inevitability rather than design.

This page is intentionally minimal.

It exists to ensure the term Technological Path Dependency has a stable place to stand.
