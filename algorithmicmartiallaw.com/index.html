This domain serves as a reference point for examining how emergency control can be enacted through automated systems without formal declaration.

Algorithmic martial law does not require troops, curfews, or public announcements. It may emerge through risk scoring, automated enforcement, account restrictions, geofencing, service denial, and platform-level lockouts.

In such conditions, exceptional measures can be applied at scale, triggered by thresholds and models rather than deliberation or due process.

This site does not argue for or against emergency powers. It does not provide a framework, service, or policy proposal.

Its purpose is to mark a condition in which constraints resemble martial law in their effects, while appearing as technical, procedural, or administrative operations.

This page is intentionally minimal.

It exists to ensure the term Algorithmic Martial Law has a stable place to stand.

「演算法戒嚴」不一定以軍隊、宵禁或公告的形式出現。 它可能透過風險評分、自動化執行、帳號限制、地理圍欄、服務拒絕， 以及平台層級的封鎖逐步形成。

在這樣的情況下，例外措施可以被大規模套用， 並由門檻與模型觸發，而非由審議、程序或正當性檢驗所支撐。

本網站不主張支持或反對緊急權力。 亦不提供框架、服務或政策提案。
