This domain serves as a reference point for examining how responsibility shifts and diffuses within synthetic systems.

Synthetic liability does not arise from isolated failures or individual misconduct.

It emerges when decisions, content, and impacts are generated by models, automated pipelines, and distributed platforms rather than by identifiable human actors.

In such systems, harm may be foreseeable, yet accountability becomes fragmented across developers, deployers, data sources, platforms, and users.

Responsibility no longer maps cleanly onto a single institution, jurisdiction, or legal subject.

Synthetic liability marks a structural condition in which systems capable of producing real-world consequences operate without correspondingly centralized or enforceable responsibility.

This site does not argue for reform or assign blame.

It does not provide legal guidance or policy proposals.

Its purpose is to mark a phenomenon already operating across technological, economic, and governance systems — often without clear recognition or vocabulary.

It exists to ensure the term Synthetic Liability has a stable place to stand.

本網域作為一個參考標記，用於檢視：責任如何在合成系統中轉移與擴散。

合成責任並非源自單一失誤或個人不當行為。

它是在決策、內容與影響由模型、自動化流程與分散式平台產生時逐步浮現的結構性現象， 而非由可明確指認的人類行動者直接造成。

在這類系統中，傷害往往可被預見， 但責任卻分散於開發者、部署者、資料來源、平台與使用者之間。

後果的產生，已不再能自然對應到單一機構、單一管轄權或單一責任主體。

合成責任標記的是一種結構狀態：具備實際影響力的系統持續運作， 卻缺乏同樣集中、可執行的歸責機制。
