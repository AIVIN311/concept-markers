This domain serves as a reference point for examining how legitimacy is generated when scoring systems, risk models, and automated approvals become procedural authority.

In this condition, decisions appear neutral while relying on feature selection, threshold design, and tolerated error ranges.

When approval becomes a default pipeline, dissent and appeal are often reduced to exceptional pathways.

This site does not advocate a position.

It does not provide a service, framework, or policy proposal.

Its purpose is to mark how the sense of legality can be output by systems rather than formed through public deliberation.

This page is intentionally minimal.

It exists to ensure the term Algorithmic Legitimacy AI has a stable place to stand.
