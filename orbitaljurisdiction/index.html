This domain serves as a reference point for examining how jurisdiction may operate within orbital infrastructures.

Orbital jurisdiction refers to the regulatory, administrative, and sovereign reach applied to satellites, stations, and space-based systems.

As orbital layers become economically and strategically significant, jurisdiction may be distributed across states, corporations, and treaty regimes.

This site does not advocate a position.

It does not provide a framework, service, or policy proposal.

Its purpose is to mark a structural condition in which governance extends into orbital layers.

This page is intentionally minimal.

It exists to ensure the term Orbital Jurisdiction has a stable place to stand.
